Research Infrastructure

SRM-ATMOS utilizes modern computational resources, atmospheric datasets, numerical models and artificial intelligence frameworks to investigate weather and climate extremes, lightning activity, atmospheric electricity and climate variability.

Numerical Modelling Facilities

WRF-ARW Modelling System

High-resolution Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) model simulations for weather forecasting, rainfall extremes and mesoscale atmospheric processes.

WRF-ELEC Framework

Simulation of thunderstorm electrification, lightning processes and atmospheric electricity using WRF-ELEC.

Climate Modelling

CMIP6 and regional climate simulations for climate variability, future projections and extreme event assessment.

Atmospheric Data Resources

ERA5 Reanalysis

Atmospheric and surface datasets for diagnostics and modelling.

GPM IMERG

Satellite rainfall estimates for precipitation monitoring.

INSAT

Satellite observations for cloud and convection studies.

WWLLN Lightning Data

Global lightning observations for thunderstorm and lightning research.

Data Analysis and Visualization

Python Ecosystem

Atmospheric data analysis using Python, NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Xarray and NetCDF tools.

GIS Applications

Spatial analysis and visualization of atmospheric and climate datasets.

Scientific Visualization

Generation of weather maps, climate diagnostics and research graphics.

Artificial Intelligence Resources

Machine Learning

Applications of machine learning for rainfall prediction, climate analysis and weather forecasting.

Physics-Informed AI

Integration of atmospheric physics and artificial intelligence for weather and climate applications.
